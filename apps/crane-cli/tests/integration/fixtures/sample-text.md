## Introduction

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define how to make web content more accessible
to people with disabilities. Accessibility involves a wide range of disabilities, including visual,
auditory, physical, speech, cognitive, language, learning, and neurological disabilities.

## Summary

These techniques are informative and do not set requirements for conforming to WCAG. The
normative conformance criteria are specified in the WCAG documents themselves.
